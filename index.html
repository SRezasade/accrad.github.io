AI 4 Radiology

We bring Radiology in areas where radiologists are not available

There is a global shortage of radiologists. Chest radiograph interpretation is critical and time-consuming. For radiologists, it can be difficult at times to diagnose with confidence under times of uncertainty and pressure. Using our AI based medical image processing platform for chest scans methodologies and implementations portrays our plaforms’s potential to combat the increasing burden and diagnostic downtime heavily dependent on a limited number of well-trained radiologists and medical experts, who must review and prioritize an increasing number of patient chest scans. The system is designed to process large numbers of chest scans per day. As a result, our AI models will help predict which patients are most likely to need a ventilator or medication and which can be sent home for self-quarantine. Thus, our platform helps the fight against COVID-19 pandemic in three ways:

  • Identification
  • Monitoring and
  • Predicting patient status.

AccRad Open Source

Open Source project to help combat 2019-nCoV with Machine Learning powered by scalable cloud infrastructure. We propose the use of Deep Neural Networks. As an initial experiment the CheXNeXt Pneumonia Detection Model was used as a baseline architecture where transfer learning was used to detect pneumonia. Secondly three different convolutional neural network architectures (ResNet50, VGGNet and DenseNet) was used to detect Coronavirus infected patients via chest radiographs.

CheXRad: Deep learning for chest radiograph diagnosis

We developed CheXRad, a deep learning algorithm to concurrently detect COVID-19 and 14 clinically important diseases in chest radiographs. With 99.91% AUC, CheXRad can diagnose certain pathologies in chest radiographs at a level comparable to practicing radiologists on metrics like accuracy, sensitivity and specificity. The big difference in the performance is speed.
